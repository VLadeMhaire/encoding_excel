Getting Started in 3 Simple Steps
Follow these steps to set up and start using the MGAA VAT Tracking System immediately.
1

Enter Company Info

Open Company Information from the dashboard. Fill in Company Name, TIN (000-000-000-000), Address, Line of Business, and Authorized Employee. Required for Excel exports.

2

Select a tracking module from the Dashboard — Vatable Sales, Vatable Purchases, Expenses, CAPEX, or others. Each module auto-calculates VAT formulas as you type.

Core VAT Formulas
These formulas are applied automatically across all VAT-registered modules. Enter the gross amount and the system handles the rest.
Net Sales / Net Purchases / Net Expenses
Enter the gross (VAT-inclusive) amount. The system extracts the VAT component automatically.
Net        = Gross ÷ 1.12
// Example:
Net        = ₱1,120.00 ÷ 1.12 = ₱1,000.00
Output Tax / Input Tax
VAT computed on the extracted net amount at the standard 12% BIR rate.
Tax        = Net × 0.12
// Example:
Output Tax = ₱1,000.00 × 0.12 = ₱120.00
Expanded Withholding Tax (EWT)
EWT computed on the net amount at the applicable BIR rate for the transaction type.
Withholding = Net × Rate
// Common BIR EWT rates:
1% Goods/Supplies   2% Services
5% Rental           10–15% Professionals

Frequently Asked Questions
Quick answers to the most common questions about the MGAA VAT Tracking System.

How do I save my work?

Use Save Data to File in the left sidebar, or press Ctrl+S. This downloads a file: .vat (Sales), .purch (Purchases), .exp (Expenses), .taxes (Taxes & Licenses), or .capex (CAPEX). Files are JSON-formatted and can be reloaded any time.

Why can't I export to Excel?

All required company fields must be filled before exporting: Company Name, TIN, Address, Line of Business, and Authorized Employee. Go to the Company Information module or fill them in the right sidebar in VAT Sales. The system will show a warning indicating which field is missing.

How do I edit multiple cells at once?

Press Ctrl+M (or click Select Multiple in the sidebar) to enter Selection Mode. Then click and drag across the cells you want to select. Use Ctrl+C to copy, Ctrl+V to paste, or Delete to clear selected cells. Press Escape to exit Selection Mode.

What is the correct TIN format?

Philippine TIN format is 000-000-000-000 (12 digits, dash-separated). The system auto-formats and validates as you type. The first 9 digits are your tax identification number; the last 3 represent the branch code (000 for the main office).

How do I create a General Journal?

Use the Journal Entry page. Load your saved transaction files (.vat, .purch, .exp, .taxes, .capex) and the system automatically consolidates all transactions into a balanced General Journal with proper Debits and Credits. Click Print Journal for a formatted printout.

What happens at high zoom levels?

The interface adapts progressively: 100–125% shows the full layout · 150% compacts headers · 175–200% shortens column labels and hides less-important columns · above 200% the system enters Table Focus Mode automatically. Use Ctrl+Shift+F at any time to manually toggle Table Focus Mode.

Is my data secure?

Yes. All data is stored locally — either in your browser localStorage or in the files you download. No data is ever transmitted to any server. The system works entirely offline after the initial page load. You are in full control of your data at all times.

How do I update company information?

Go to the Company Information module from the Dashboard. Update your details and click Save & Sync. The data is written to localStorage and automatically loads into the VAT Sales right panel and all Excel report headers.

How are VAT amounts calculated?

Enter the gross (VAT-inclusive) amount. The system extracts VAT automatically using: Net = Gross ÷ 1.12 and VAT = Net × 0.12. This is the standard BIR method for VAT-registered taxpayers. EWT is computed as Withholding = Net × Rate where the rate depends on the transaction type (1% goods, 2% services, 5% rental, 10–15% professionals).
