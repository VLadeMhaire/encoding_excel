Getting Started
New to the system? Follow these steps to be up and running in minutes.
Initial Setup — 3 Steps
1

Enter Company Information

Go to Company Information from the Dashboard. Fill in Company Name, TIN (000-000-000-000), Business Address, Line of Business, and Authorized Employee. This is required before you can export to Excel. Data auto-saves to browser localStorage.

2

Choose a Tracking Module

Select any module from the Dashboard — VAT Sales, VAT Purchases, VAT Expenses, and more. Click Add New Row (or press Ctrl+N) to start entering transactions. All VAT formulas calculate automatically as you type the gross amount.

3

Save & Export

Press Ctrl+S to save your work as a local file (.vat, .purch, .exp, etc.). When ready, press Ctrl+E to export a formatted, BIR-ready Excel report with your company header, transaction table, and VAT summary.

100% Offline — Your Data Never Leaves Your Device

The MGAA VAT System stores all data locally in your browser or in files you save. No data is ever transmitted to any server. The system works completely offline after the initial page load.

Module Guides
A brief overview of each module and what it does. For deeper guides, visit the Quick Guide.
Company Information
Required before exporting

Store your business name, TIN, address, line of business, authorized employee, and report date. This information appears on every Excel export header and syncs to the VAT Sales right panel automatically.

Required Fields

Company Name · TIN · Address · Line of Business · Authorized Employee. Excel export will be blocked until all five are filled.

Journal Entry
General ledger consolidator

Loads your saved transaction files (.vat, .purch, .exp, .taxes, .capex) and automatically generates a complete General Journal with Debit and Credit entries. Editable fields allow manual adjustments; totals update in real time. Use Print Journal for a formatted printout.

Supported Formats

.vat · .purch · .npurch · .rpurch · .exp · .nexp · .taxes · .capex

VAT Sales / Purchases / Expenses
Core VAT tracking modules

Enter gross (VAT-inclusive) amounts

Net and Output/Input Tax are computed automatically: Net = Gross ÷ 1.12, Tax = Net × 0.12.

Select withholding tax rate

Choose from common BIR rates (0%, 1%, 2%, 5%, 10%, 15%) depending on the nature of transaction. Withholding = Net × Rate.

TIN auto-fill

Type a previously used TIN and the system auto-fills the customer/supplier name and address. All TINs are stored in the TIN Library.

Save and Export

Ctrl+S saves a .vat/.purch/.exp file. Ctrl+E generates a formatted Excel report with company header, transaction table, customer/supplier summary, and distribution charts.

Non-VAT Purchases & Expenses
VAT-exempt transactions

Used for purchases and expenses from VAT-exempt suppliers or on zero-rated items. Transactions are recorded at gross value without VAT extraction. Saves as .npurch and .nexp files respectively. Can be loaded into the Journal Entry consolidator.

Capital Expenditures (CAPEX)
Asset acquisition tracking

Track vatable and non-vatable asset acquisitions. Vatable assets: Net = Gross ÷ 1.12; non-vatable assets use gross value directly. Input tax on capital goods (ITCG) is tracked separately. Saves as .capex files.

Taxes & Licenses
Government payments and fees

Record BIR annual registration fees (₱500), documentary stamp tax, permit fees, and other government dues. Each entry supports Cash or Charge payment mode. Saves as .taxes files for audit trail and journal consolidation.

Purchase Returns
Returns with VAT reversal

Track goods returned to suppliers. The system automatically calculates the VAT adjustment: Net Return = Gross ÷ 1.12. This ensures proper input tax credit reversal in your journal. Saves as .rpurch files.

VAT Formulas & Rates
Standard BIR computation methods used across all VAT-registered modules. Enter the gross amount — everything else is automatic.
Net Amount (VAT Extraction)

Enter the gross (VAT-inclusive) amount from the invoice. The system extracts the net amount automatically.

Net        = Gross ÷ 1.12
// Example:
Net        = ₱11,200.00 ÷ 1.12 = ₱10,000.00
Output Tax / Input Tax (12% VAT)

VAT is computed on the extracted net amount at the standard 12% BIR rate.

Output Tax = Net × 0.12
// Example:
Output Tax = ₱10,000.00 × 0.12 = ₱1,200.00
Expanded Withholding Tax (EWT)

EWT is deducted by the payor on the net amount at the BIR-prescribed rate for the transaction type.

EWT = Net × Rate
// Example (2% services):
EWT = ₱10,000.00 × 0.02 = ₱200.00
1%Goods / Supplies
2%Services
5%Rental (real & personal property)
10–15%Professionals (doctors, CPAs, lawyers)

VAT Registration Threshold

Businesses with gross annual sales or receipts exceeding ₱3,000,000 are required to register as VAT taxpayers under the TRAIN Law. Below this threshold, the 3% Percentage Tax applies instead.

localStorage Auto-Save

In addition to file saves, the system automatically persists data in your browser’s localStorage after every change. This means your data is preserved even if you accidentally close the tab — just reopen the module and it will restore automatically.

Clearing Browser Data

Clearing your browser’s cache or site data will erase localStorage. Always save a .vat/.purch/.exp file before clearing your browser. Company info and TIN library are also stored in localStorage.

TIN Library
Central customer & supplier registry

The TIN Library stores all customer and supplier TINs, names, and addresses from your transaction data. When you enter a TIN in any module, the system auto-fills the name and address from the library. Access the TIN Library from the Dashboard nav or the TIN Library link.

Troubleshooting
Solutions to common issues. If your problem isn’t listed here, check the FAQ or contact support.
Export to Excel is blocked
1

Fill all required company fields

Go to Company Information and ensure all five fields are filled: Company Name, TIN, Address, Line of Business, and Authorized Employee.

2

Click Save & Sync

After filling the fields, click Save & Sync in Company Information. Then return to the module and try exporting again.

3

Check for data in the table

The export requires at least one data row in the table. Add a row with a gross amount entered.

Data disappeared after closing the browser

localStorage can be cleared by browser updates, private/incognito mode, or manual cache clearing. To prevent data loss:

1

Always save a file before closing

Press Ctrl+S after each session to download a .vat/.purch/.exp file to your computer. Files are the permanent backup.

2

Do not use Incognito / Private mode

Incognito mode clears localStorage when the window closes. Use a regular browser window for the system.

3

Reload from file

Press Ctrl+O to load a previously saved file and restore your data.

TIN auto-fill is not working

TIN auto-fill pulls from the TIN Library stored in localStorage. If it’s not filling: (1) make sure the TIN was previously saved by entering and saving at least one transaction with that TIN, (2) check that you’re typing the TIN in the correct format 000-000-000-000, and (3) open the TIN Library to verify the entry exists.

VAT amounts look incorrect

The system always assumes the amount you enter is VAT-inclusive (gross). Make sure you are entering the total invoice amount including VAT, not the net amount. If you enter the net amount by mistake, the output tax will be over-calculated.

// Correct: enter VAT-inclusive (gross)
Gross = ₱11,200 → Net = ₱10,000, Tax = ₱1,200
// Wrong: entering net as gross
Gross = ₱10,000 → Net = ₱8,929, Tax = ₱1,071
Frequently Asked Questions
Quick answers to common questions.

How do I save my work?

Press Ctrl+S at any time, or click Save Data to File in the sidebar. This downloads a file with the correct extension for the module you’re in (.vat, .purch, .exp, etc.). Files are JSON and can be reloaded with Ctrl+O.

Where do I set up my company details?

Go to Company Information from the Dashboard. Fill in all fields and click Save & Sync. The data syncs to the VAT Sales right sidebar and all Excel export report headers automatically. Without these details, Excel export will be disabled.

How do I edit multiple rows at once?

Press Ctrl+M to enter Selection Mode. Then click and drag across the cells you want to select. Use Ctrl+C to copy, Ctrl+V to paste values, or Delete to clear the selected range. Press Escape to exit Selection Mode.

What TIN format should I use?

Use the format 000-000-000-000 (12 digits with dashes). The system auto-formats as you type. Digits 1–9 are the unique tax number; digits 10–12 are the branch code (000 for the main office).

How do I generate a General Journal?

Open the Journal Entry module. Load your saved transaction files by clicking the load buttons for each section (Sales, Purchases, Expenses, etc.). The system automatically consolidates all entries into a balanced General Journal with proper Debits and Credits. Click Print Journal to print or save as PDF.

Why doesn’t my company info appear in VAT Sales?

Make sure you clicked Save & Sync in the Company Information page. Then open or refresh VAT Sales — the system reads company info from localStorage on every page load. If it still doesn’t appear, try pressing Ctrl+Shift+R (hard reload) in the VAT Sales page.

Does the system work without internet?

Yes. After the initial page load (which loads fonts and icons from Google/CDN), the system works completely offline. All calculations, data entry, saving, and exporting happen entirely in your browser with no server communication.

The table is too small at high zoom — what can I do?

Press Ctrl+Shift+F to enter Table Focus Mode. This hides all panels except the table, giving it maximum screen width. Press again to restore the full interface. The system also adapts automatically at 150%, 175%, and 200% zoom levels by compacting the UI.
